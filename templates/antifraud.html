Fraud detector:

Insurance fraud in motor claims is a significant issue affecting both insurance companies and policyholders. Fraudulent claims lead to increased premiums, financial losses, and a burden on legal and investigative resources. These fraudulent activities can take various forms, such as staged accidents, exaggerated damages, or false injury claims. Identifying potential fraud cases early is crucial to minimizing financial risks and improving the efficiency of claim processing.

To address this challenge, I have developed a machine learning-powered web portal designed to predict the probability of fraud in motor insurance claims. The platform enables users to enter key details regarding:

  • Vehicle Details (e.g., make, model, category, price)
  • Policy Information (e.g., policy number, start date, deductible, policy type)
  • Accident & Claim Details (e.g., collision date, accident location, police report status, witness presence)

Once the required data is submitted through the form, the system processes the information and sends a request to an external API for fraud probability analysis. The external API utilizes pre-trained machine learning models that analyze patterns in historical claims data to determine the likelihood of fraudulent activity.

By leveraging machine learning and external data processing, this web portal provides a data-driven, efficient, and scalable approach to fraud detection. It enhances decision-making for insurers, helping them identify suspicious claims faster and more accurately.
